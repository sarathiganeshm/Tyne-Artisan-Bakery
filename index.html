Our story

We started because we couldn't find decent bread.

Honestly. Tom came back from six months in France, tried every bakery in Newcastle, and kept ending up disappointed. Not dramatically, just that low-level sadness of bread that's slightly wrong. So he started making his own. In a flat.

Jen took one taste, told him it was the best thing she'd eaten in a year, and suggested they sell it at the Quayside market. They sold out in 40 minutes. Someone asked if they did subscriptions. They said yes before thinking it through.

That was March 2024. There are three of them now. One oven. One starter (named Frank — he's been going since January 2024 and is doing well). They still sell at the Quayside every Sunday, but now you can get the bread without leaving your house.

"We don't have any big plans for scale. We just want to make the best loaf we know how to make, as many times as we can manage, and get it to people while it's still worth eating." — Tom & Jen

The numbers, honestly

561 subscribers as of February 2026. Started with 93. Frank the starter was a good investment.
48hr minimum cold ferment. Most supermarket bread is done in two hours. You can taste the difference.
8mi delivery radius. We're not trying to ship to Edinburgh. We want you to get it the same day we bake it.
1 oven. Three bakers. No shortcuts. That's pretty much the whole business model.

Slow fermentation

48 hours minimum. The flavour develops properly, the gluten structure breaks down, and your gut thanks you for it. You can't rush it and we don't try.

Actually local

Within 8 miles of Newcastle. In-store collection with 15% off for subscribers who'd rather just come in. Free delivery when your order is over £30.

Properly small

Three people. One oven. One starter called Frank. No plans to franchise, no plans to sell to a supermarket. Just bread, made right, while we can manage it.
